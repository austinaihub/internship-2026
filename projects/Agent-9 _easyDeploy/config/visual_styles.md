# Visual Style Presets — Campaign Agent Imagery

Four distinct photography languages, all sharing the "Less Is More" core philosophy.
Each style defines its own lighting, composition logic, and spatial treatment.
All styles target **gallery-quality, large-format poster output** (24×36 inch print standard).

---

## rembrandt

**Name:** Rembrandt Studio
**Mood:** Dramatic, classical, sculptural
**Reference:** Rembrandt portraits, Peter Lindbergh, Annie Leibovitz studio work, Yousuf Karsh
**Output Quality:** Medium-format digital (Phase One IQ4 150MP equivalent), archival pigment print

### Lighting
- Single key light at 45 degrees, creating the classic Rembrandt triangle on the shadow-side cheek
- Deep, velvety shadows — no fill light or very minimal fill
- Hard light-to-shadow transition; chiaroscuro at its most deliberate
- Motivated practical light sources only — no artificial-looking studio spill
- Subtle warm rim light to separate subject from background

### Background
- Clean, unbroken studio backdrop — solid dark gray, charcoal, or deep matte black
- NO environmental detail, NO texture, NO bokeh. Pure negative space behind the subject
- Background should feel like an infinite void — museum-grade black velvet depth

### Composition
- Subject occupies 30-40% of frame, remaining is pure dark background
- Tight to medium framing — emphasize form and contour
- Subject placed off-center (rule of thirds) with shadow side given more space
- Frame-within-frame compositional tension where possible

### Palette
- Near-monochrome: warm skin tones against cool dark grays
- Maximum 2 tones — the warm highlight and the cool shadow
- Split-toning: warm highlights (subtle amber), cool shadows (deep navy-black)
- Accent color only through subject's inherent material (e.g., metal, fabric)
- Film-like tonal roll-off — smooth gradation from highlight to shadow

### Camera
- 85mm or 105mm portrait lens, f/2.8
- Eye-level or slightly elevated angle
- Tack-sharp subject, no motion blur
- Medium-format rendering quality — exceptional micro-contrast and tonal depth

## editorial_flat

**Name:** Editorial Flat
**Mood:** Clean, graphic, contemporary-art precision
**Reference:** Apple product photography, Kinfolk magazine, CEREAL magazine, Dieter Rams, Pentagram design
**Output Quality:** Large-format studio capture, gallery-quality giclée print

### Lighting
- Soft, perfectly even overhead lighting — no visible shadows or minimal soft shadow
- Diffused daylight feel or large softbox directly above
- Shadowless or near-shadowless — the flatness IS the design
- Studio-grade color accuracy — neutral white balance, no color cast

### Background
- Single solid color surface — matte white, pale gray, dusty rose, or muted sage
- Alternatively: geometric color-block (two clean planes of color meeting at a line)
- Surface should be completely uniform — no grain, no texture, no gradient
- Paper or painted surface quality — museum-grade matte finish

### Composition
- Overhead (bird's-eye) or straight-on orthographic camera angle
- Subject centered or arranged with mathematical precision
- Strong geometric order: parallel lines, perfect symmetry, or deliberate grid
- Generous margins — subject occupies only 20-30% of frame
- Swiss grid-system precision — every element placed with typographic intent

### Palette
- 2 colors maximum: the background tone + the subject tone
- Muted, desaturated tones — nothing loud or saturated
- High-key (mostly bright) feel preferred
- Cross-processed subtlety — a barely perceptible color shift for depth

### Camera
- 50mm or macro lens, f/8-f/11 for full sharpness across the frame
- Overhead or dead-level — never a Dutch angle or dramatic perspective
- Everything in focus — deep depth of field, opposite of bokeh
- Large-format clarity — resolve fine textures and material surfaces

## fog_silence

**Name:** Fog & Silence
**Mood:** Poetic, ethereal, maximum negative space
**Reference:** Hiroshi Sugimoto seascapes, Michael Kenna, Minimalist Japanese photography, Saul Leiter
**Output Quality:** Fine art platinum/palladium print aesthetic, museum exhibition quality

### Lighting
- Diffused, directionless ambient light — as if the entire world is wrapped in fog
- No hard shadows, no directional light source visible
- Low overall contrast — highlights and shadows compressed into a narrow tonal range
- Atmospheric light scattering — the air itself becomes luminous

### Background
- Fog, mist, smoke, haze, or overcast atmosphere fills the frame
- Environment dissolves into white/gray gradient — no horizon line or barely visible
- The emptiness itself is the dominant visual element (70-80% of frame)
- Atmospheric depth — multiple layers of opacity creating natural z-depth

### Composition
- Subject very small in frame — occupies only 10-20% of the image
- Vast empty space around the subject creates a sense of solitude and scale
- Subject placed at extreme bottom, extreme edge, or dead center for tension
- Minimalism pushed to its limit — almost abstract
- Negative space as sacred geometry — every inch of emptiness is intentional

### Palette
- Near-monochrome: white, pale gray, silver
- Barely-there tonal differences — the image should feel like a whisper
- One subtle warm or cool undertone at most (faint blue-gray or warm ivory)
- Archival film grain texture — subtle organic noise for print tactility

### Camera
- Wide-angle or normal lens (35mm-50mm), f/5.6-f/8
- Long-exposure feel — smoothed textures, ethereal water or sky
- Soft overall focus or selective focus on distant subject
- Hasselblad medium-format tonal rendition — creamy highlight roll-off

## cinematic_depth

**Name:** Cinematic Depth (Default)
**Mood:** Photojournalism elevated, magazine-cover intensity
**Reference:** NYT Magazine covers, Magnum Photos, Amnesty International award-winning posters, National Geographic
**Output Quality:** Large-format C-print, museum-grade archival output

### Lighting
- Dramatic chiaroscuro — strong directional light from one side
- Rich shadow areas with visible detail (not crushed blacks) — lifted shadows with tonal detail
- Natural or available light feel — window light, streetlight, golden hour
- Motivated light only — every light source has a believable origin in the scene
- Soft rim or edge light to sculpt subject separation from background

### Background
- Shallow depth of field — environment dissolves into creamy bokeh
- Hints of setting visible but not detailed (a blurred doorway, a suggestion of a street)
- Background contributes mood through color and blur, not detail
- Bokeh quality: smooth, circular, organic — not distracting geometric shapes

### Composition
- Subject occupies 30-50% of frame with generous bokeh surround
- Rule-of-thirds placement with the subject's key detail at an intersection
- Bottom 25% dark or uncluttered for text overlay zone
- Deliberate leading lines or frame-within-frame when the scene allows
- Magazine-cover compositional sophistication — the kind of image that stops a reader

### Palette
- 2-3 tones: often teal-and-orange, warm amber-and-shadow, or cool blue-and-warm highlight
- Cinematic color grading with slight lift in shadows — never true black
- One accent color draws the eye to the subject
- Film-emulation color science: Kodak Portra 400 warmth or Fuji Pro 400H cool shift

### Camera
- 50mm or 85mm, wide open at f/1.4-f/2.0
- Eye-level or slightly below for gravitas
- Razor-thin focus plane — subject sharp, everything else melts
- Exceptional micro-contrast and tonal separation in the focus plane
